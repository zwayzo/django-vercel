History of the Korean Language

Korean is the official language of both North Korea and South Korea, with dialects spoken in Korean communities around the world. The Korean script, known as Hangul, was created in the 15th century by King Sejong the Great and is celebrated for its scientific design and simplicity.

The Rich Culture of Korean-Speaking Regions

Korean culture is deeply rooted in traditions such as Confucianism, Buddhism, and Shamanism. From the bustling streets of Seoul to the serene landscapes of Jeju Island, South Korea offers a blend of modernity and tradition. Korean cuisine, famous for kimchi, bulgogi, and bibimbap, reflects the country's agricultural roots and culinary innovations.

Learn Korean

Learning Korean opens doors to understanding Korean dramas, music (K-pop), and cinema, as well as exploring the country's history and technological advancements. With over 75 million speakers worldwide, Korean is a gateway to connecting with diverse cultures and making new friendships.
