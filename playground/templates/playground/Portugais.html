History of the Portuguese Language

Portuguese is a Romance language originating from the Iberian Peninsula. It is the official language of Portugal, Brazil, Mozambique, Angola, Guinea-Bissau, Cape Verde, São Tomé and Príncipe, and East Timor. The language evolved from the Latin spoken by the Romanized inhabitants of the region after the Roman conquest of the Iberian Peninsula in the third century BC. Over the centuries, Portuguese spread to various parts of the world through exploration and colonization, becoming a global language with diverse dialects and accents.

The Rich Culture of Portuguese-Speaking Countries

Portuguese-speaking countries boast a rich cultural heritage with significant contributions to music, literature, dance, and cuisine. From the soulful Fado music of Portugal to the vibrant Carnival of Brazil, the culture is as diverse as the countries themselves. Portuguese literature has produced world-renowned authors like Fernando Pessoa, José Saramago, and Paulo Coelho. The architectural beauty of cities like Lisbon, Rio de Janeiro, and Luanda reflects a blend of historical influences and modern innovation.

Learn Portuguese

Learning Portuguese opens up opportunities to communicate with over 250 million people worldwide. It allows you to explore the rich cultural tapestry of Portuguese-speaking countries, understand their history, and enjoy their literature, music, and films in the original language.
