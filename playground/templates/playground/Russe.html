History of the Russian Language

Russian is an East Slavic language and an official language in Russia, Belarus, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, and more. It belongs to the family of Indo-European languages and is written in the Cyrillic script. The language has a rich literary tradition with renowned writers such as Tolstoy, Dostoevsky, and Pushkin contributing significantly to world literature.

The Diverse Culture of Russian-Speaking Countries

Russian-speaking countries have a diverse cultural heritage spanning music, art, dance, and literature. From the classical ballet performances of the Bolshoi Theatre to the iconic architecture of Moscow and St. Petersburg, Russian culture reflects a blend of historical influences and modern creativity. Russian literature, music, and cinema continue to influence global culture.
