History of the French Language

French is a Romance language of the Indo-European family. It descended from the Vulgar Latin of the Roman Empire, as did all Romance languages. French evolved from Gallo-Romance, the spoken Latin in Gaul, and more specifically in Northern Gaul. Its closest relatives are the other langues d'oïl—languages historically spoken in northern France and in southern Belgium, which French (Francien) has largely supplanted.

The Rich Culture of France

The French culture is rich and varied, with a long history of contributions to art, literature, fashion, and cuisine. From the Eiffel Tower in Paris to the lavender fields in Provence, France offers an abundance of beautiful sights and experiences. French art and architecture are celebrated worldwide, and the French language itself is considered one of the most beautiful and romantic languages in the world.

Learn French

Learning French can open up a world of opportunities. Whether you want to explore the rich literary tradition, enjoy French films and music, or communicate with millions of speakers worldwide, learning French is a rewarding experience. French is not only spoken in France but also in many countries across the globe, including Canada, Belgium, Switzerland, and several African nations.
